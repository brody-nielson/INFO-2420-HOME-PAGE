In the Beginning

Rent A Pet began with a family that loved dogs. George Smith and his wife Carol owned two Irish Setters. These dogs were pets to the Smith family children and extended family members.

One day as George was walking his dogs in the park, he noticed how they attracted the attention of passing women. A young woman remarked that she would love to have a big dog like that to walk, but she had a small apartment and couldn't keep a large dog.

This brief encounter sparked an idea. What if people wanted companionship of pets without the responsibility of ownership? Perhaps people would be willing to rent a pet for a short period of time.

Advantages of the Business

  • Renters could have access to the pet at their leisure
  • Renters could pick the pet they wanted
  • Renters could exercise with the pet
  • Renters could enjoy companionship
  • No upkeep or medical expenses for renters
  • The owners could enjoy many pets

Disadvantages of the Business

  • The business must provide housing and food
  • The business must cover medical expenses
  • Liability insurance is required
  • The owners are responsible for daily care

Where We Are Today

After weighing the pros and cons, the Smiths started Rent A Pet in the fall of 1980. The business offered companionship without the cost or responsibility of pet ownership.

The company began by renting dogs only, but has since expanded to include cats, horses, pigs, and snakes.

What Makes Us Unique

Rent A Pet has grown from its original Provo location and now operates franchises in Logan, Bountiful, Salt Lake City, and St. George.

The company employs its own veterinarian and animal trainer. Pets are trained daily, and Rent A Pet has never had an accident involving rental animals.

Customers include senior citizens in retirement homes, young adults seeking companionship, entertainers, and party hosts. Snakes are often rented for Halloween or shock-value events.
